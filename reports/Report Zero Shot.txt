Structured Report: Automotive Industry Cyber Threats
 
Executive Summary
 
This report provides an overview of recent cyber threats and incidents affecting the automotive industry, including data breaches, ransomware attacks, and cyberattacks. The report covers key details such as threat actors, affected systems, impact, and recommended mitigations.
 
Threat Details
 
1. Hyundai AutoEver America, LLC Data Breach
   - Threat: Unauthorized access to sensitive customer information.
   - Affected Data: Names, Social Security numbers, driver’s license information.
   - Timeline: February 22, 2025, to March 2, 2025.
   - Threat Actor: Unspecified.
 
2. Motility Software Solutions Data Breach
   - Threat: Data breach exposing sensitive information of approximately 760,000 customers.
   - Affected Data: Names, dates of birth, driver's license numbers, Social Security numbers.
   - Timeline: August 19, 2025, publicly disclosed on September 12, 2025.
   - Threat Actor: Unspecified.
 
3. Data I/O Ransomware Attack
   - Threat: Ransomware attack disrupting critical operational systems.
   - Affected Systems: Shipping, manufacturing, production systems.
   - Timeline: Began on August 16, 2025.
   - Threat Actor: Unspecified.
 
4. Nidec Corporation Cyberattack
   - Threat: Cyberattack involving the theft of sensitive data.
   - Affected Data: Internal documents, business partner letters, procurement documents, labor safety policies, contracts.
   - Timeline: Early June 2024.
   - Threat Actors: 8BASE and Everest.
 
5. Jaguar Land Rover Ransomware Cyberattack
   - Threat: Ransomware cyberattack disrupting retail and production activities.
   - Affected Systems: Global applications and IT systems.
   - Timeline: Recent incident.
   - Threat Actor: Unspecified.
 
Impact
 
1. Hyundai AutoEver America, LLC Data Breach
   - Impact: Exposure of sensitive customer information, potential risk of identity theft and fraud.
 
2. Motility Software Solutions Data Breach
   - Impact: Exposure of sensitive information of approximately 760,000 customers, potential risk of identity theft and fraud.
 
3. Data I/O Ransomware Attack
   - Impact: Disruption of critical operational systems globally, material impact on financial results and condition.
 
4. Nidec Corporation Cyberattack
   - Impact: Theft of sensitive data, potential risk of industrial espionage and competitive disadvantage.
 
5. Jaguar Land Rover Ransomware Cyberattack
   - Impact: Severe disruption of retail and production activities, potential financial losses and operational downtime.
 
Recommendations
 
1. Hyundai AutoEver America, LLC Data Breach
   - Mitigations: Terminated unauthorized access, engaged third-party cybersecurity specialists, offered complimentary two-year credit monitoring and identity protection services.
   - Recommendations for Affected Individuals:
     - Regularly review financial account statements and credit reports.
     - Report any discovered fraud or identity theft to financial institutions and relevant authorities.
     - Place fraud alerts or establish security freezes on their credit files.
 
2. Motility Software Solutions Data Breach
   - Mitigations: Awareness of potential risk of identity theft, legal rights, monitoring financial accounts and credit reports, credit protection measures.
   - Recommendations for Affected Individuals:
     - Contact Schubert Jonckheer & Kolbe LLP for information about legal rights and potential entitlement to money damages and injunctions.
     - Monitor financial accounts and credit reports for any suspicious activity.
     - Consider placing a fraud alert or credit freeze with credit bureaus.
 
3. Data I/O Ransomware Attack
   - Mitigations: Implemented containment measures, engaged cybersecurity experts and advisors.
   - Recommendations:
     - Engage cybersecurity experts and advisors to mitigate the impact and restore affected systems.
     - Notify potential data breach victims after completing a third-party investigation.
 
4. Nidec Corporation Cyberattack
   - Mitigations: Enhance VPN security, regular security audits, employee training, data encryption, incident response plan, monitor dark web.
   - Recommendations:
     - Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for VPN access.
     - Conduct regular security audits to identify and mitigate vulnerabilities.
     - Provide cybersecurity training to employees.
     - Ensure sensitive data is encrypted both at rest and in transit.
     - Develop and regularly update an incident response plan.
     - Use tools and services to monitor the dark web for any leaked company data.
 
5. Jaguar Land Rover Ransomware Cyberattack
   - Mitigations: Proactively shut down affected systems, investigate the extent of the breach, gradually restart global applications, communicate with stakeholders, implement robust cybersecurity measures.
   - Recommendations:
     - Proactively shut down affected systems to mitigate the impact.
     - Work to understand the extent of the breach and whether any data has been compromised.
     - Gradually restart global applications in a controlled manner.
     - Keep stakeholders informed about the incident and the steps being taken.
     - Implement robust cybersecurity measures to prevent future attacks, such as regular system updates, employee training, and incident response planning.
 
Additional Context
 
- Data I/O: The second company this week to report a ransomware attack to the SEC. Cybersecurity firm Dragos reported 657 ransomware attacks on industrial entities globally between April and June 2025, with the manufacturing sector being the hardest hit.
- Jaguar Land Rover: Part of a string of recent cyber and ransomware attacks targeting various industries, including insurance and airline sectors. The FBI has warned about the cybercriminal group "Scattered Spider" targeting the airline sector.
 
This structured report provides a comprehensive overview of recent cyber threats and incidents affecting the automotive industry, along with recommended mitigations and additional context.
